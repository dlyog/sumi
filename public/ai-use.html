Trust and transparency

AI use and local data notice

Understand which material is AI-assisted, what the local installation records, and how to question or correct it.

AI-assisted material

Lesson illustrations and narration may be generated with local AI tools before release. They are saved with the application; opening a lesson does not call an image or audio model. The information icon on each lesson visual shows its prompt and model.

AI output may be inaccurate. Illustrations are conceptual, not photographs of physical qubits. Verify important scientific, investment, legal, or professional decisions independently and report misleading material from the lesson page.

Notice at collection

The local installation may collect identifiers and activity needed to operate and improve the course: account name and email, a random browser identifier, pages viewed, lesson reactions, voluntary accuracy reports, subscription choice, and scheduled improvement jobs.

  • Purpose: authentication, saved learning workflows, aggregate audience measurement, content review, and service security.
  • Storage: the configured local PostgreSQL database or an in-memory development store.
  • Sale or sharing: 1StopQuantum does not implement sale of personal information or sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • Sensitive information: do not enter health, financial, government identifier, precise location, or other sensitive data in feedback.
  • Retention: the operator should retain activity only as long as needed for the stated purposes and delete test data before public deployment.

Privacy choices

Depending on the law and deployment, users may request access, deletion, or correction from the operator of their installation. This application does not implement sale or sharing of personal information. Identity verification may be required. See the repository-local privacy notice.
